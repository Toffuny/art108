Introduction

A screenshot of Canabalt, one of the games that was played during the video game lab

For the video game lab in class, my partner & I played 3 games: canabalt, magical maiden madison, and a dance of fire and ice. Canabalt and a dance of fire and ice in particular have the connection of being a game that the structure of the game is the same: both have a screen that moves along with the player’s avatar (that only needs to be controlled by one simple action). Canabalt only required players to use one key to make their players jump over the obstacles that will come on screen while a dance of fire and ice had users just click their mice on the beat to advance forward on the squares. These simple controls made it easy to dive into the game and for us (the players) to understand the game even without much instruction. The screen moving horizontally not only processes the game at a set pace (rather than letting the player take their own time), but also gives the players pressure to keep them engaged. As for the third game we played, magical maiden madison, is a completely different kind of game compared to the other two. This game is a visual novel game, therefore players follow along to the game’s story and make their own decisions to ultimately choose their ideal ending. Magical maiden madison has those play as a girl, that lives a double life as a superhero and a normal schoolgirl, respond to her friend’s text messages. It had a different vibe compared to the other two as there wasn’t any action or appealing visuals and relied heavily on the storytelling.
