Wizards Unite


Blog Post #7

For our last class the professor asked us to pair up and play the mobile game Harry Potter: Wizards Unite around campus. The game, released by Niantic, is essentially like the company's other game Pokemon Go but with everything being Harry Potter themed instead; it also has a few components in it that lets players make more choices than in the Pokemon counterpart. The mechanics of the game are pretty straight forward: you wander around the real world to save people and collect items in the virtual one. To fight against an enemy, players needs to trace the presented pattern as quickly and as accurately as possible. The better the traced line, the more damage is dealt to the enemy. My peers and I had an overall positive experience of the game despite the fact that none of us were very knowledgeable about the franchise. Having others around helped us figure out the meaning behind collecting the energy items as well as choosing one thing out of the sea of identical looking objects. I feel like playing with peers for games, such as this one where you have to move around to gain more things, makes it a lot more fun. Games such as Wizrds Unite was made for community interaction.
