Kickin' it Old School


Blog Post #1

For experiencing the internet arcade, I chose to play the 1981 game Kick from the wide selection of available games. I had no prior experience in playing this game, but what I essentially understood from it is that the player is a clown on a unicycle and the objective of the game is to follow the commands (displayed at the bottom of the screen) concerning the columns of balloons up above. The player can also kick the balloons to have them bounce back up and thus resulting in bonus points. If even one balloon touches the ground, it automatically equates to a life; therefore, you only have three chances to mess up. One thing I discovered playing the game was that I didn’t initially understand it at all, and when I did, it still took a while to get the hang of it. I ended up not progressing as far as I would’ve liked ( I couldn’t go past level 3) and the reason I failed was because I couldn’t reach the balloons in time before they reached the ground. I’m not too sure if there is a way to speed up the character, but my inability to find it caused me to die a lot more than I would’ve liked.
The fact that I played this classic arcade game on an emulator does not compare to how the original would be. I feel like the experience would be entirely different if played on the original hardware as its presence and interactivity alone emerges you more into the game; the decorations on the hardware in a player’s peripheral vision as well as having physical buttons to smash immerses them into the game’s world, magic that playing on an emulator (which left me feeling indifferent at the end) just can’t emulate. The classic 1981 arcade game of Kick was an interesting game to play, just not one that I was good at.
