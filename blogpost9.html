Course Reflection


Blog Post #9

Alas the class is finally over after a fun-filled semester. The Art 108 game studies class was particularly interesting because you got to learn the basics of how a game is made (the planning, iteration, etc.) through the process of doing it ourselves. The fact that the class was structured in a way that my classmates & I were able to see the creation process for board games and video games (as well as having the freedom to explore other game type options as we pleased) first hand by making our own instead of just sitting through lectures every class session. On the days that we did have lectures, the material that was covered came in handy during the playtesting process of our games. In the end, we all did in fact "Read about games, write about games, play games and design games" through means of trying our hands at the game making process from coming up with the concept, prototypting, failing and then having to try again, and then finally succeeding. As someone who typically likes things to be a one-and-done, this class really taught me that that's not actually as efficient as I think it is. That going thorough the long process with no shortcuts results in a much higher quality product.
